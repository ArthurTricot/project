What is the Cornell Method

The Cornell Method involves structuring a notebook with an index for quick reference. It includes sections for task lists, goal setting, and scheduling, with space for note-taking and project planning. Mind mapping and reflection pages aid in brainstorming and reviewing progress. There's a focus on breaking goals into actionable steps and incorporating reference material. Personalization is encouraged to fit individual preferences and workflows. Regular review ensures staying on track with tasks and goals.
